Traditional VPN vs Zero Trust

Aspect Traditional VPN Zero Trust (Cloudflare)
Security model Perimeter (trusts anyone inside the local network) Constant verification of identity and device (MFA)
Performance High latency (all traffic goes through a single point) Optimized speed thanks to Cloudflare's global network
Access control All or nothing (full access to local subnet) Granular (specific access per authorized application)
Multi-factor (MFA) Complex to integrate in traditional routers Native and mandatory during identity validation
User experience Slow manual reconnection after micro-cuts Transparent and automatic connection with WARP client

FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between VPN and Remote Desktop?

A VPN is an encrypted corridor that safely binds an external PC to the office network. A Remote Desktop (RDP) session lets you control an actual office computer remotely.

Do I need to buy new laptops for my team?

No. We can set up our secure client software and connection protocols on your existing workstations (Windows/macOS) easily.

Will VPN connections slow down performance?

We utilize optimized encryption standards and data compression techniques to ensure high speed and low latency for database operations.

Need to modernize your IT infrastructure?

Tell us what system you currently have and we will help you define a secure, scalable cloud, hybrid, or remote access solution.
